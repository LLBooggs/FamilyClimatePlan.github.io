🏠   Does Buying a Home Change the Climate Math?
What renting limits

As a renter you can act on 6 of the 15 items in this plan. The 9 homeowner actions are locked — you can't specify the HVAC system, add insulation, install solar, or plant trees. And those 9 actions include some of the highest-impact, longest-duration levers available to a household.

The most consequential: specifying a heat pump at HVAC replacement. That single decision eliminates combustion from your home and locks in decarbonization for 15–20 years. Missing the replacement window means 15–20 more years of unnecessary emissions from equipment you didn't choose.

The nuance

Your EV purchase is still your biggest immediate lever — and it's fully available right now as a renter. Don't wait.

Most homeowner actions trigger at equipment end-of-life. Buying a home with mid-life HVAC means waiting for the replacement window before acting on the most impactful items. The climate case for buying is strongest if the home has aging systems you can replace soon.

What you buy matters as much as whether you buy. A well-insulated newer rental may have a lower footprint than an old drafty house you own. Target homes with aging HVAC, a solar-ready roof, and south/west exposure for trees — so Phase 2 is actionable within 2–3 years of purchase.

The IRA residential credits are real and available now — 25C (heat pumps, insulation, windows), 25D (solar), 30D (EV) — and they make the homeowner actions more financially accessible than at any prior point in history.

When the HVAC system needs replacement, specify all-electric heat pumps rather than gas equipment. Heat pumps provide both heating and cooling at 3× the efficiency of resistance heating. Missing this window means 15–20 years of unnecessary emissions.

A mature oak on the southwest corner of a Tulsa home can eliminate the need for afternoon mechanical cooling on mild days.
